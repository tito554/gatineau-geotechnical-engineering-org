Methodology and scope

Gatineau sits at 45.43° north latitude where frost penetrates up to 1.8 meters in open ground. That changes everything about compaction timing and moisture control. The Proctor test establishes exactly how much water your soil needs to reach peak density. Too dry and the particles won't knit together. Too wet and you're pumping pore pressure with every pass. We test material from your actual borrow source or excavation. The lab report includes the full moisture-density curve, not just the peak. You can see the sensitivity of your soil and adjust field compaction windows around Gatineau's short construction season. Modified Proctor applies 56,000 ft-lbf/ft³ of compactive effort. Standard Proctor uses 12,400 ft-lbf/ft³. The right choice depends on your specification. We help you match the test to your project requirement without overspending on unnecessary lab work.

Local ground factors

The National Building Code of Canada references compaction acceptance based on Proctor density. In Gatineau, inspectors enforce it strictly. They know the local glacial till and Champlain Sea clays. They know what passes and what doesn't. A missing Proctor curve on a subgrade or utility trench backfill triggers a stop-work order. Retesting delays the project. Rework costs multiply. We see it most often in late-season projects pushing to finish before freeze-up. The soil is wet. The compactor is running. But nobody checked if the moisture is within range of optimum. The Proctor test answers that before it becomes a problem. For structural fill under footings or slab-on-grade in the Hull sector, skip the lab curve and you're gambling with differential settlement. The cost of the test is negligible against the cost of tearing out failed compaction.

Common questions

How long does a Proctor test take?

Standard turnaround is 24 to 48 hours from sample drop-off. We can expedite to same-day results for active earthworks operations where compaction is waiting on the lab curve. Call ahead to coordinate.

Do I need Standard or Modified Proctor for my project?

It depends on the specification and the loading. Residential footings and utility trench backfill typically follow Standard Proctor. Road base, structural fill under commercial slabs, and heavy civil work usually require Modified Proctor. We can review your geotechnical report and advise.

How much soil do you need for a Proctor test?

We need approximately 25 to 30 kg of representative material. The sample must be bagged and sealed immediately after excavation to preserve natural moisture content. For granular soils, a larger sample of 40 to 50 kg is preferred to account for oversize correction.
