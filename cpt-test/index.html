CPT Testing in Gatineau: Continuous Soil Profiling for Western Quebec Geology

Sound ground. Sound decisions.

LEARN MORE

The CPT rig working in Gatineau is a 20-tonne tracked unit fitted with a 15 cm² piezocone that records tip resistance, sleeve friction, and pore pressure simultaneously at 2 cm intervals. It pushes through the sensitive Leda clays and glacial tills that define the Outaouais corridor, with a hydraulic ram that can penetrate dense basal till at depths exceeding 25 metres. For projects near the Ottawa River, where alluvial silts overlie Champlain Sea deposits, the cone's u₂ filter element provides the dissipation data needed to estimate consolidation rates before foundation design begins. The truck-mounted configuration allows access to tight lots in the Hull sector without dismantling the push system between soundings.

A single CPT sounding in Gatineau's Champlain clays replaces about four boreholes of SPT sampling for stratigraphic resolution, cutting field time by more than half.

Methodology and scope

Gatineau sits on a complex Quaternary stratigraphy where the Champlain Sea inundated the region roughly 11,000 years ago, depositing the thick sequence of silty clays that now dominates the low-lying areas of Hull and the Gatineau sector. These soils exhibit pronounced sensitivity — values above 30 are common — which means the remoulded strength measured by the CPT sleeve friction can drop to a fraction of the peak value.
For sites near the Gatineau Hills transition, the cone encounters interbedded glacial till that demands high push forces, a condition where we often pair the CPT with seismic refraction to map the bedrock surface before choosing rig capacity.
The pore pressure parameter Bq derived from the u₂ reading helps identify the depth of the groundwater table, a critical input for slope stability analysis when excavating for underground parking near the escarpment. In sandy lenses found at the base of the clay sequence, the normalized friction ratio provides a reliable check against published liquefaction susceptibility criteria under the NBCC seismic provisions.

Local ground factors

In Gatineau, many times we see that contractors underestimate how quickly the bearing layer drops from the Rockcliffe escarpment toward the river plain. A site on Chemin de la Montagne might show refusal at 4 metres in till, while three blocks south toward Boulevard Saint-Joseph the same refusal depth exceeds 22 metres because the bedrock surface plunges beneath a deep paleochannel. Without a continuous CPT trace, a designer could misinterpret a dense sand lens as bearing stratum and undercut the pile length by several metres. The consequence shows up later as differential settlement between the wing and the tower of a multi-family building, particularly when the clay contains sensitive fabric that loses strength upon remoulding during excavation.

How does CPT compare to boreholes for Gatineau's sensitive clays?

CPT provides a continuous vertical profile of soil response without the sample disturbance that affects standard penetration testing in sensitive Leda clays. The pore pressure measurement captures the in-situ stress state, which is critical for evaluating the risk of strength loss during construction. Boreholes remain useful for retrieving specimens for laboratory Atterberg and triaxial testing, so the two methods often complement each other on large projects.

Can the CPT rig reach refusal in Gatineau's glacial till?

Yes, the 20-tonne tracked rig typically reaches refusal on the dense Precambrian-derived till that underlies much of the Gatineau area. Refusal is defined as a sustained tip resistance exceeding 50 MPa or an inability to maintain the standard penetration rate. At that point the sounding provides a reliable indicator of the bearing stratum depth.

What NBCC site class information does a seismic CPT provide?

A seismic CPT measures downhole shear wave velocity (Vs) directly, which delivers the average Vs30 value used to assign NBCC site classes from A (hard rock) to E (soft soil). In Gatineau, many sites with deep Champlain Sea clay fall into class D or E, which amplifies the design spectral acceleration and must be accounted for in the structural analysis.
